Yoga

The Sanskrit word yoga has the literal meaning of "yoke", from a root yuj meaning to join, to unite, or to attach. It is a union between physical, mental and spiritual discipline. These disciplines are between the mind and body; mind, body and breathe; Higher Power and soul; spirit, mind and body, etc. Yoga is any spiritual path that may lead from suffering and the circle of reincarnations to liberation. Yoga Avani teaches Ashtanga, Hatha, and Power Vinyasa Flow yoga.

Ashtanga Yoga is the eightfold spiritual path to obtain the control of the mind, the state of samadhi (spiritual state of consciousness) and eventually liberation. The following eight angas (limbs) are the stages of the spiritual path: Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratayahara, Dharana, Dhyana and Samadhi.

Hatha Yoga is the yoga of "effort" and part of Raja Yoga, the yoga of the "mind".

Vinyasa Yoga is the linking of breath-synchronized body movements used to transition between sustained postures.

There are many yoga styles of yoga, all acceptable to lead to the same goal of liberation.

Breathing

Pranayama is a Sanskrit word meaning "extension of the prana or breath" or more accurately, "extension of the life force". The word is composed of two Sanskrit words, Prāna, life force (the breath) and "ayāma", to extend or draw out. Pranayama Yoga is the art of breathing control. It calms the mind; lowers the blood pressure; eases migraines; improves cardiac functions; helps with all ailments, improves respiratory (asthma) issues; energizes the mind; overcomes sleepiness and laziness; improves lung capacity; relieves stress & anxiety; prepares for meditation; balances the body, mind and breath, etc.

Yoga Avani practices the following Pranayama techniques on a regular basis:

  • Nadi Suddi “Energy Channel Purification”
  • Suryabedana
  • Ujjayi “Victorious”
  • Bastrika “Bellows of Blacksmith”
  • Bramari “Humming Bee”
  • Shitali
  • Shitkari
  • Natural Breathing
  • Abdominal Breathing
  • Thoracic Breathing
  • Clavicular Breathing
  • Full Yogic Breathing

About

Yoga entered Regina's life nearly 15 years ago. She is certified by Yoga-India/Abhyasa Yoga Shala, one of the 20 top training centers worldwide by yoga.info, in Mysore, India. After years practicing in San Francisco, she decided to take her yoga studies to a deeper level and traveled to Mysore, India to study with Master Bharat Shetty.

Regina inspires and transforms individuals and groups through movement, chant, prayer and conversation. She also teaches studio classes, corporate yoga, smaller intimate yoga classes, retreats and via SKYPE in various styles and levels. Along with yoga, Regina creates her own balance by teaching spin classes, creating mobile applications, and a veteran independent hi-tech software consultant.
